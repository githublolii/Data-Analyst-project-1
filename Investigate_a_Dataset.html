Project: Investigating the TMDb Movie Dataset

Introduction

In this project, I am analyzing the TMDb Movie Dataset. This data contains details on about 10K movies, including their budgets, revenues, and popularity. My goal is to figure out what factors make a movie financially successful.

Questions for Analysis:

  1. Budget vs. Revenue: Is there a real link between how much a studio spends and how much they earn?
  2. Runtime Distribution: How long is the "average" movie? (1D Analysis)
  3. Genre Trends: Which genres are being produced the most, and which ones are the biggest earners?
  4. Popularity influence: Does a high popularity score on TMDb actually mean the movie made more money? (2D Analysis)

Data Wrangling

The data here is not clean and it needs to be cleaned before I can analyze it. Many movies have 0 listed for budget or revenue, which is impossible. I will also remove columns that are not helpful for my specific questions.

My Plan for Cleaning:

  • Delete unnecessary columns: Removing things like id, imdb_id, and homepage.
  • Remove duplicates: Ensuring every movie is only counted once.
  • Handle Zero Values: Replacing 0 with NaN in the financial columns and removing those rows.
  • Fix Dates: Converting the release_date column to a standard datetime format.
In[]:
# 1. dropping the extra columns
cols_to_remove = ['id', 'imdb_id', 'homepage', 'tagline', 'overview', 'keywords', 'cast', 'director']
df.drop(columns=cols_to_remove, inplace=True)

# 2. getting rid of duplicate rows
df.drop_duplicates(inplace=True)

# 3. fixing the 0 values
df['budget_adj'] = df['budget_adj'].replace(0, np.nan)
df['revenue_adj'] = df['revenue_adj'].replace(0, np.nan)

# dropping those NaN rows now
df.dropna(subset=['budget_adj', 'revenue_adj'], inplace=True)

# 4. fixing the date column
df['release_date'] = pd.to_datetime(df['release_date'])

print("Shape after cleaning:", df.shape)

Reducing Repetitive Code

To meet the project requirement, I have created a function called make_labels. This function will handle the titles and axis labels for every plot I make, so I don't have to write the same code over and over.

In[]:
def make_labels(t, x, y):
    """
    Simple function to add a title and axis labels.
    """
    plt.title(t, fontsize=14)
    plt.xlabel(x, fontsize=12)
    plt.ylabel(y, fontsize=12)
    plt.grid(axis='y', linestyle='--', alpha=0.5)
    plt.show()

Conclusions

Summary of Findings:

  • Financials: My analysis shows a strong positive correlation between budget and revenue. Spending more money often results in higher returns.
  • Popularity: High popularity scores on TMDb are a good indicator of financial success.
  • Genres: While Drama is the most produced genre, Adventure and Animation movies actually earn the most money on average.
  • Length: Most successful movies have a runtime of about 100 minutes.

Limitations of my Analysis:

  • Dropped Data: I had to delete thousands of rows because they had 0 for budget or revenue. This means my results are biased towards bigger movies that actually report their money stats.
  • Genre Issues: I only used the first genre listed. Many movies fit into multiple categories, which my analysis doesn't capture.
  • Correlation vs Causation: Even though budget and revenue are correlated, spending money does not cause success. Other factors like script quality and marketing also matter.

Suggestions for Future Research:

In the future, I would like to look at the Return on Investment (ROI) to see which genres are the most "efficient" at making money, not just the ones that make the highest total revenue.
